Deep Learning for Medical Image Analysis

This project explores the application of deep learning techniques to medical image analysis, with a focus on cancer diagnosis and vascular image segmentation. Through these studies, I investigated how neural networks can assist clinicians in extracting meaningful information from complex biomedical imaging data.

Research Motivation

Medical imaging plays a central role in disease diagnosis and treatment planning. However, manual interpretation is often time-consuming and subject to variability. Deep learning offers an opportunity to improve efficiency, consistency, and diagnostic accuracy through automated image analysis.

Focus Areas:
  • Medical Image Classification
  • Image Segmentation
  • Attention Mechanisms
  • Deep Learning for Healthcare

Project 1: Breast Cancer Classification

Developed a dynamic multi-scale fusion framework with attention mechanisms for breast cancer diagnosis from medical imaging data.

Methods

  • Multi-scale feature extraction
  • Attention-based feature fusion
  • Deep convolutional neural networks
  • PyTorch implementation

Results

  • Accuracy: 0.87
  • AUC: 0.92

The study demonstrated that incorporating multi-scale information and adaptive attention mechanisms improved classification performance and enhanced model robustness.

Project 2: Vascular Segmentation

Developed deep learning models for automatic vascular segmentation from cardiovascular imaging datasets.

Methods

  • Image preprocessing and augmentation
  • Deep learning-based semantic segmentation
  • PyTorch training pipelines
  • Quantitative performance evaluation

Results

  • Dice Score: 0.84
  • mIoU: 0.76

The project focused on accurately identifying vascular structures from complex imaging data to support downstream biomedical analysis and clinical research applications.

Future Directions

Future work may explore multimodal learning approaches that integrate imaging, clinical, and genomic information to improve disease characterization and support precision medicine applications.
